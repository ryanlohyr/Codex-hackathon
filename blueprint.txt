## Visual Style
**Theme: dark** — a deep-space aesthetic maximizes contrast for faint starfields, lensing arcs, and subtle boundary cues (event horizon vs shadow), while making the accretion disk glow and relativistic effects feel immersive.

**Palette (semantic roles)**
- **background**: `#050814` (near-black blue; space backdrop)
- **primaryGlow**: `#34d399` (cyan-green; photon paths & “physics overlays”)
- **diskHot**: `#f59e0b` (amber; inner hot accretion region)
- **diskCool**: `#fb7185` (pink-red; cooler outer disk / emission gradient)
- **boundaryHighlight**: `#60a5fa` (soft blue; horizon/photon-sphere cues & lensing arcs)

---

## Learning Goal
Understand how a black hole’s gravity shapes what we see—creating a **shadow**, defining an **event horizon**, forming a **photon sphere**, distorting background stars via **gravitational lensing**, and producing observable relativistic effects like **time dilation** and **tidal stretching**.

---

## Analogy
**A heavy bowling ball on a trampoline covered with glow-in-the-dark marbles**: the ball represents the black hole; marbles are photons—some curve around, some orbit briefly, and some fall in—while the fabric’s steepness hints at how time and paths “tilt” near the hole.

---

## Visual Concept
**Overall scene**
- A central, nearly black circular **shadow** region, surrounded by a thin, luminous **event horizon boundary cue** (subtle ring/edge marker distinct from the shadow).
- A slightly larger, brighter ring marking the **photon sphere** region (a “danger orbit” band where light can loop).
- A glowing **accretion disk** around the black hole:
  - **Inner region**: brighter and more yellow/white-hot (diskHot), visually indicating higher temperature.
  - **Outer region**: redder/pinker (diskCool), dimmer, thicker-looking.
  - Disk brightness and thickness respond to accretion rate.
- A distant **starfield** background. Near the black hole, stars appear warped into **arcs** and occasional **partial rings** (Einstein ring-like features), strongest near the photon sphere region.
- Several illustrative **photon paths**:
  - Some bend and escape to the viewer.
  - Some loop around (near photon sphere) before escaping.
  - Some cross the horizon cue and terminate (capture).
- **Infalling matter blobs/streamlines** spiral inward in the disk plane; a fraction visibly plunge past the horizon cue (disappearing).
- A **probe object** (small spacecraft marker) on an approach trajectory:
  - As it nears the horizon, it stretches along the radial direction (spaghettification cue).
  - A **probe clock/pulse beacon** emits ticks/pulses; a **distant observer clock** sits in a corner HUD. The probe pulses appear to slow relative to the distant clock as the probe gets closer.

**Spatial arrangement**
- Black hole centered.
- Disk is a tilted ellipse depending on viewing inclination; the “near side” appears brighter (optional Doppler-like emphasis as a visual hint, not necessarily physically exact unless desired).
- Starfield fills the background; lensing arcs concentrate around the black hole.

**Motion/animation**
- Accretion disk rotates smoothly; inner region rotates faster than outer to suggest differential rotation.
- Infalling blobs gradually spiral inward; occasional “plunge” events past the horizon cue.
- Photon paths can animate as traveling light pulses showing direction and fate (escape/loop/capture).
- Probe moves with user-controlled distance; stretching and time dilation effects update continuously.

**What changes with user controls**
- **Mass**: increases size scale of shadow/horizon cues and strengthens lensing; photon bending becomes more dramatic.
- **Accretion rate**: thickens/brightens disk and increases infalling matter density; hotter inner glow becomes more prominent.
- **Viewing inclination**: rotates disk from face-on (near circular) to edge-on (thin line); lensing arcs and apparent disk shape adjust.
- **Probe distance**: moves probe inward/outward; increases tidal stretching and time-dilation difference as it approaches the horizon.

## Info Points
1. **Label:** EVENT HORIZON  
   **Explanation:** The event horizon is the boundary where escape becomes impossible—once anything crosses it, it cannot return or send signals back out.  
   **Near:** the thin boundary cue ring just outside the shadow region  
   **Color:** boundaryHighlight `#60a5fa`

2. **Label:** SHADOW  
   **Explanation:** The shadow is the dark region formed because many light paths are captured; it’s larger than the event horizon because gravity bends incoming light.  
   **Near:** the central black area  
   **Color:** background `#050814`

3. **Label:** PHOTON SPHERE  
   **Explanation:** A region where gravity is strong enough that light can orbit briefly; tiny changes decide whether a photon escapes or falls in.  
   **Near:** the brighter ring outside the horizon cue  
   **Color:** primaryGlow `#34d399`

4. **Label:** ACCRETION DISK  
   **Explanation:** Hot gas and dust spiral inward, heating up from friction and compression; the inner disk glows hotter and brighter than the outer disk.  
   **Near:** the rotating disk structure  
   **Color:** diskHot `#f59e0b` (with diskCool `#fb7185` gradient)

5. **Label:** GRAVITATIONAL LENSING  
   **Explanation:** The black hole’s gravity bends light from background stars, creating warped arcs and partial rings around the hole.  
   **Near:** the starfield distortion arcs surrounding the black hole  
   **Color:** boundaryHighlight `#60a5fa`

6. **Label:** TIME DILATION  
   **Explanation:** Clocks closer to the black hole run slower relative to a distant observer; signals from near the horizon appear increasingly slowed.  
   **Near:** the probe clock/pulse indicator and the distant observer clock HUD  
   **Color:** primaryGlow `#34d399`

## Scaffolding Steps
1. **Instruction:** Set `inclinationDeg` to **0–10** and observe the disk and shadow from face-on.  
   **Concept:** Viewing angle changes the *apparent shape* of the accretion disk and how symmetric the lensing appears.  
   **Condition:** `inclinationDeg <= 10`

2. **Instruction:** Increase `blackHoleMass` to **30** and compare lensing arcs and photon bending to the default.  
   **Concept:** Greater mass strengthens gravitational bending and enlarges the characteristic shadow/horizon scale.  
   **Condition:** `blackHoleMass >= 30`

3. **Instruction:** Raise `accretionRate` to **0.7+** and identify the hotter inner region versus cooler outer region.  
   **Concept:** Accretion controls disk brightness/thickness and highlights that inner regions are hotter due to stronger gravity and faster motion.  
   **Condition:** `accretionRate >= 0.7`

4. **Instruction:** Move the probe inward to `probeDistanceRs` **≤ 4** and watch the probe pulses compared with the distant clock.  
   **Concept:** Gravitational time dilation increases strongly as distance decreases; signals “slow down” near the horizon.  
   **Condition:** `probeDistanceRs <= 4`

5. **Instruction:** Bring the probe to `probeDistanceRs` **≤ 2.6** and observe tidal stretching and the “point of no return” cue.  
   **Concept:** Tidal forces grow rapidly near the black hole, and crossing the horizon marks irreversible capture even if the shadow boundary looks similar.  
   **Condition:** `probeDistanceRs <= 2.6`
